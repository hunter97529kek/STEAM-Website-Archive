About VERC
Who are we, what are we doing, and who we'd like to thank..

Who?
You might know me from such shows as... er... A long long time ago when Worldcraft was still in a beta version, I had a website called the Worldcraft Editing Site. I don't even remember the url but it certainly isn't there anymore. It was one of those free university web accounts. That turned into The Forge when Loki (the original one, dammit) and I joined forces. Through community and industry involvement the site evolved into something I was and am quite proud of.

In 1998, Worldcraft's focus was turned to Half-Life, and in November of that year the Half-Life Editing Resource Center was born. The primary goal of that site was to provide information related to Half-Life mapping. Between then and now, the Half-Life mod community has taken off to a greater degree than anyone could have hoped. This, combined with Valve's upcoming products, necessitates a site that can accomodate all of this additional content while still remaining easy to use. Enter the Valve ERC.

What?
The goal of the Valve ERC, much like the Half-Life ERC, is to provide you with all the information you need to stay current with your mapping needs for Valve's games (and their mods). The site content is summarized in the Site Map.

Thanks!
First, to our gracious hosts at Speakeasy.net and Edward Bender and Lisa Phillips in particular. This site was made possible thanks in large part to the help provided to me by Richard Smith (Square Eight, Chatbear, Quake3Rally). David Speyrer, of course, is responsible for the slickness of Worldcraft (and Ben Morris before him, as the original author). Last, there's Valve, for their great games and outstanding support of the user community.
