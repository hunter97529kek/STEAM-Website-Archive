Cumbersome
by Dietz
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: TFC
number of players: 12 - 32
download: cumbersome.zip

The most important part of any map is the design. This counts double for TFC maps. They require a certain balance to make them fun to play, and enjoyable to both defend and attack. Pulling this off is not as easy as you might think. Many things must be considered, more than I have time to mention in the introduction to this review. If someone does manage to get these things correct, than you can be sure to be in for a treat.

Gameplay consists of a flag in the middle of the map, and two teams that must battle it out to claim possession of it. Upon picking up the flag you must carry it to the capture point, which will transfer it to the top of your base. For every 30 seconds that you can keep it there, you rack up the points. This means you won’t be spending your whole time cap hunting, defence plays a big part and if you want to win, I suggest you put away your Rambo shoes and play as a team. Defending the flag is no easy task, due to the twisting and turning multiple routes it takes a lot of man power to keep the enemy down. This is not to say that you will spend your whole time defending, for when the enemy gets the flag you will want to get it back as quickly as possible. This means getting into the enemy base and using those multiple routes to surround the flag from all sides. It’s the beauty of this map, the perfect balance of gameplay.

Due to the layout of the map and its staying away from the 2fort style, means that games aren’t decided through sniper wars. This isn’t something that I would commonly mention, but due to the overwhelming and growing problem of it I feel it’s worth pointing out that no such problem exists in this map. The one thing that I think could be improved with the gameplay, however, is putting some more signs up to document where you’ve to go. I found much of my time was spent wandering, trying to find things. This is of course the same with every map when playing it for the first time, but in TFC its nice when the advantage of signs help the newbies.

Visually the map is impressive, there’s no doubt about that. But architecturally there are places which could be made a bit more exciting. Nothing was boxy or over-scaled, it was just a bit too plain. This however, was made up for in other areas I’ll discuss in a bit. The textures used were well applied and were good choices, nothing felt out of place and all added to the overall feel of the map. The r_speeds were kept to a low with the peak being about 400.

For this map things work a little differently. Usually the visuals are basically the architecture and the textures, while the lighting comes under ambience. This time, however, lighting fits in to both categories. Put simply, this is the first map I’ve seen in a long time which managed to use a fair amount of coloured lighting without making me regurgitate my dinner from last Tuesday. In fact, the lighting was executed so well it really brought the map together and made any areas that might have been plain suddenly look so much better and at the same time creating an ambience rarely seen in TFC maps. The map being set at night allowed for a lot of shadows, but didn’t make any places annoyingly dark. Sounds were used in a standard way for a TFC map. When capping the flag for example.

There was also some other nice touches through out. One that comes to mind, that justified me writing another paragraph at the end of the review, was a couple of lights in each base that signified which team had the flag or whether it was still in the centre. Helpful to say the least as it stopped people making unnecessary trips, or at least it did me.

FINAL WORD
I’m about two superlatives away from writing “this map r0x0rz!!!”, so just click download.

RATING: 8.3
visual: 9
ambient: 8
gameplay: 8
All ratings out of 10.0
