OpForCTF_Junction
by Morlam
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: Op For CTF
number of players: 6 - 14
download: op4ctf_junction.zip

After a long time of having this map kicking around, and having sticks full of problems trying to get it to work I was relieved to finally be able to load it up and see it. I was also glad to see that time and bother hadn’t gone to waste, and I’m sure Morlam will feel the same way in about 2 minutes.

The map, like Morlam’s last has a story to it. So to give some background about the maps setting and such I’ll simply cut ‘n’ paste.

"The battle for control of Black Mesa took to the subways as civilians advanced on the Central Transit Junction. With control of the Junction, the armed refugees commanded a sizable advantage over the soldiers. The military could not allow this to happen, and in retaliation set up headquarters not far from the civilian camp. A confrontation loomed as military recon squads unkowingly sped intoa head on collision with a Black Mesa strike force..."

As you might expect from a CTF map the idea is to steal the enemies flag and return it to your base. However, there is a twist in this map. First you must shut off the power that protects the flags. Only then can you get your paws on it. This is one of the better parts of the map. In making you do this, it promotes teamplay as it can be very difficult for the same man to both turn off the power and get the flag. Although I will point out that this could have been made better if more things was effected by the loss of power. For example if some sort of siren was to go off, lights start flashing and other things that would alert the team that the base had been invaded.In doing this the gameplay would have been made faster and more exciting, as people rush to stop the enemy invaders. There is a large space available for clans to practice strategies on this team, with enough depth for them to try and time the power going off and the flag grabbing.

Visually the map is very similar to Riverstation. The corridors are architectually the same, and this is in no way a bad thing. There was however some brush problems, and more specifically one piece of wall which extended out in the middle of a passageway, easily fixed but something that should have been noticed before hand. Texturing is never out of place, and makes it plainly obvious which base your in. For the most part it sticks with the typical HL concrete greys, but nevertheless it ain’t too shabby.

One of the nice points of the gameplay is using the trains to your advantage. Nothings more fun than killing people while moving at high velocity. Something else which could be utilized by well-organised teams, having someone pick up the flag carrier on a well timed train could greatly improve chances of a cap.

The lighting was very plain, with no areas really showing a great deal of shadows or creating a mood. It was a very Half-Life affair and could have been made much more interesting. The best use of light was when a train was coming round to the front of a base, a light would come on and warn everyone in the area. Sounds were only really used when capping the flag. However, sounds will play a part in the gameplay due to the train. You can hear it long before you can see it.

  • Add sounds and lighting effects for when the power goes off to improve ambience and gameplay.
  • Fix small brush problems
  • Improve lighting throughout

FINAL WORD
All the people who like to kill and fight over a piece of cloth tied to a stick could do a lot worse than pick up this map. Not fantastically brilliant, but altogether a well made, and fun map.

RATING: 6.7
visual: 7
ambient: 6
gameplay: 7
All ratings out of 10.0
