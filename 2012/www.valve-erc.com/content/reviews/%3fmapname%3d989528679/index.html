HL Noir
by TTJ
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: HLDM
number of players: 3 - 8
download: hlnoir.zip

OK, so you came into see why it’s a real classic and why it looks dated? Well, it all lies in the unique idea presented in this map. You see, its all in Black and White. No, not that snazzy game from Lionhead, but the colours. It was an interesting idea to use for a HLDM map but it turned out great.

Although gameplay isn’t the best thing about this map it’s in no way bad. The fire fights are usually close quarters and the shotgun freaks among you should enjoy the map. There is one specific area which will see most of the action and it will become apparent after some play. Weapon placement is well thought out and the decision to miss the egon was a good one. The RPG is available in between some groovy moving walkway things. Although the gameplay is fun there’s nothing special. There are no areas where vertical fire fights take place and there is little variation to sustain interest. Connectivity was good with moving between areas easily done and a weapon always within sight.

Graphics are where this map shines. The idea to have all black and white textures turned what could have been a regular mediocre map into an ingenious mediocre map. What I first expected when hearing about this map all that time ago was a bunch of boring, dull, grey textures. When entering the game I discovered that the HL textures and been converted excellently and everything just looked so hip. The map was something that could have very easily turned out to be regurgitated poo, but in the end was pulled off very well. Apart from textures, the architecture in the map was very good as well. Most doorways were arches and any corners were nicely curved making everything look silky smooth The map is consistently tasy throughout in the graphics department, walls that would normally be flat have little details in them to spice things up. It may also be a good idea to look up every now and again as the lights are often geometrical wonders.

Ambience is a difficult one to judge in this map. Being in black and white the lighting is odd, but is definitely noticable. Above all else the lighting is tailored to fit with the rest of the map, complimenting the textures well. Sounds aren’t used in the map.

FINAL WORD
Some very pleasant(ville) gameplay and a great twist with the graphics mean anyone should be able to dig this map. You knew I couldn’t resist.

RATING: 7.0
visual: 8
ambient: 6
gameplay: 7
All ratings out of 10.0
