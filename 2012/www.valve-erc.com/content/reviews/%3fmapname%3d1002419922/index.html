De_Eggfart
by Mazy
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: CS Bomb Defuse
number of players: 4 - 8
download: de_eggfartnight.zip

The Counter-Strike community has always prided itself on the maturity of it’s members. Players and mappers alike have always shown themselves to be responsible and business-like. Mazy once described himself to me as a “big player” in the Counter-Strike community, so it would seem fitting that he would continue this tradition. Yes ladies and gentleman, De_Eggfart is in fact named after a day in Mazy’s life and does contain giant Pringles tubes and Snickers boxes. Score!

This was a sort of unexpected review, being that I had just uploaded one earlier in the day (read the review of Huey here) , the map had only just released earlier today, and because Mazy didn’t submit the map to the Valve-ERC. Yet, when I saw Mazy spamming a link to the map and a server of the IP address all over the IRC room floor I knew that it was something I didn’t want to miss, having seen the screenshots and all. I downloaded the map, and jumped into the server to be greeted with the fantastic score of 3 kills and 11 deaths. Certainly didn’t look like Mazy was going to butter me up for a good review.

I proceeded to play on, and couldn’t help but notice the giant presence of Pringle tubes. There was one point in the game where I had a very weird thought, here I was sitting on top of a giant Pringles tube shooting an M4A1 Colt at someone standing at the window of a distant building. Mazy is insane, or alternatively Bagpuss slipped something in my Sprite, and this time it was laxatives. Despite the shear oddness of what I was seeing before me I was having fun. The maps layout was very inter-connected and the maps smallness made it very fast, and very exciting. You never knew where the enemy would appear from, as it was easily possible for them to have spotted you and then darted away to appear from behind in just a few seconds. This was the cause of my death more than a few times, in fact it was the cause of my death so many times by the time I left I had bound ‘k’ to shout profanity at my killer. I was really starting to get involved the Counter-Strike communities maturity.

Visually the map is sound, with good texturing and architecture. What stole the show though was, of course, the custom textures. The giant Pringles looked like giant Pringles, as did the Twix bars scattered on every corner. With these things included the map felt like pop art, I keep wondering if maybe I missed the giant Campbells soup tin.

Ambience in this map is suprisingly good, and this seems like a perfect time to mention something. There is two versions of this map, one set at night and one during the day. This review is based on the night version, and by clicking the download link up top that’s just what you’ll get. But if you feel like catching some rays, wait till the end of the show and we’ll give you a link to where you can get the sunny version. Delight. The lighting is great in the night version, with shadows nicely cast and good mood created by them without hampering gameplay. There isn’t any areas that are too dark, and there aren’t any which are overly bright. Nice warm colours are also used instead of boring, annoying, white light.

Sound is used in this map, which is something I commonly get excited about. Enough at least to make me attempt to dance. This time however, it made me scared. Scared of how frickin’ big that cricket must be. Anywhere you are in the map you can hear it, and it would seem likely that it was this cricket that left it’s afternoon snacks littered about town. Is it a bird? Is it a plane? No! It’s a giant cricket wielding Twix bars with Pringle crumbs all over his T-Shirt.

FINAL WORD
According to Mazy he named the map after what he was up to the day he made it, this certainly must be the case. For this is one map that definetily does not smell of poo.

Catch the daylight version at this page.

RATING: 7.0
visual: 7
ambient: 6
gameplay: 8
All ratings out of 10.0
