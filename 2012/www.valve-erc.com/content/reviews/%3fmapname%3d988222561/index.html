1closer
by SYmPATRIC
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: HLDM
number of players: 4-10
download: 1closer.zip

Upon loading the map I took a walk around, and as with everything you make a first impression. As one great person in history said, “The problem with first impressions are, you only get to make one.” My first impression with this map was of the fine architecture, but I’m glad I only got to make one. As I’m sure if I got to make another, it wouldn’t have the same effect.

The architecture shows that some effort was put into making the map. There is never a square room and never a boring wall. A large amount of the walls were made at odd angles, always slanted and odd. This of course makes the map more interesting; no one wants to play a map where all rooms are square and all corners are at 90 degrees to one another. Along with architecture runs textures – in this map, like so many HL maps, they were gray and dull. They don’t look necessarily bad, but it would be nice to see some variety in the texture department to match up to architecture. Apart from the odd architecture, the other thing I noticed was the large amount of water. There were pools of it everywhere -- between cracks in pathways or just shallow pools on platforms. It made the map look a lot nicer because of it and made things more interesting. Thankfully r_speeds stay at low level despite the funky architecture, they peak at about 800 but that's only in low traffic areas. Generally most people shouldn't have a problem.

The map was set out over multiple levels. The top level is outside and contains some good position for long range sniping at lower sections. The lower level can be accessed by any of the two staircases or simply jumping down one of the holes. Because of the layout, gameplay is kept varied between close range and long range, and also includes vertical firefights. Above all else, the gameplay is fun – without which the map would be useless.

Ambience was the down side to the map. Apart from the thunder sound rumbling now and again – which was very nice – and some funky lighting on the lower level, there was really nothing topside to make the map more exciting. In my opinion the map would have looked better at night. Always just plain and uninspired. Try and include some dark corners, and maybe tinker with a little colored light. As long as you don’t go overboard on it, it could be your best friend.

FINAL WORD
Inspired architecture and some fun, varied gameplay which is disappointingly let down by a lack of ambience and atmosphere.

RATING: 6.3
visual: 6
ambient: 5
gameplay: 8
All ratings out of 10.0
