River Station
by Morlam-TPF
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: Op For DM
number of players: 5 - 10
download: op4_riverstation1_1.zip

There are some basic points I should mention first. The first and most noticeable thing is it’s for Opposing Force, it’s not often I see a custom map for it. The other is that the map could be a single player mission. It has the story, not exactly Stephen King, but it’s there. Even more so, it has that oh so clichéd “Core A” and “Core B” part to the map with directions showing you how to get there. Then as a final piece of evidence, its even set as part of the Black Mesa Research Facility!

The layout, design and the general concept behind this map are the biggest problems. The rooms and areas in which the map is based are never fun to play in. The reason most people play maps are to have fun, and without it a map is useless. With Opposing Force comes some weapons which add some welcome change to the normal HL bunch. The most fun in this map was using the electro gun firing thingy (the technical name) to shoot the water scattered throughout, instantly electrocuting the happy swimmers in an often messy explosion of the red juicy stuff. Yum.

Architecture is OK but not anything to write home about. You don’t really notice it for the most part, and the only time I did was in a corridor that had nicely curved walls. More effort could have been put in to the rock faces in the water tunnels as they were too square and just seemed like normal rooms. As for textures, there are some really bad choices scattered about. Some of the ones chosen are contrasting and make the map much more ugly than it had to be. The only misaligned textures I notices would have been hard or impossible to avoid. The first being a part of the rocky tunnels where the roof is cone shaped, leaving triangular shaped faces which are very hard to texture well. The second is in the same place and is where the nice scrolling water turns the corner. I don’t really see a way this could have been avoided so I’m not going to complain too much.

The lights were pretty standard. There were no funky effects used and there were never any really dark areas, everything was just plain. I know I’ve mentioned this in nearly all my reviews, but it can add a lot of atmosphere to your map if you have a dark corner there, a flickering light here and a broken light somewhere round that corner. The big complaint here is the coloured lighting, however. It’s overused far too much in this map, in the way it’s used about twice. Coloured lighting may have been a cool, hip addition to a game engine way back in the days of games past, but now a days people should realise that its just plain ugly. If your going to use coloured lighting, do it sparingly. The only time I could possibly want to see that blood red is either spewing from my latest frag or maybe a siren or something. Having said that, when making lights it can be a good idea to have them slightly yellow tinted rather than plain white. There were a few sounds used in the map. Rushing water, a generator hum and an echo effect to be more exact. They helped add to the area in which the map was set and it was nice to see someone bother with them.

There were some nice touches in the map. For example when entering the snipers nest a little sign outside lights up, alerting everyone of your presence. Of course it’s easily confused by jumping out the window, then upon re-entering the light will go off with you inside. The other nice thing I liked was the lever at one of the sewer entrances. Upon pulling it will leave your victims below swimming with the fishes.

FINAL WORD
An overall flawed map with an SP setting and some ugly tendencies. However, slightly redeemed by nice touches and an obvious effort with sounds.

RATING: 5.3
visual: 6
ambient: 4
gameplay: 6
All ratings out of 10.0
