CS_Instinct
by The D
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: CS Hostage Rescue
number of players: 8 - 14
download: cs_instinct.zip

Simple maps don’t always mean limiting gameplay oppurtunities. As seen in both De_Dust and 2fort a simple layout can promote teamwork, and allow a player to feel instantly comfortable with their surroundings. As long as there are chances for the different playing styles, then 7 different multi-axis routes aren’t necessary.

‘The D’ either knows this, or stublmed ass backwards on to it in the creation of his map. By looking at the map from above you see clearly that it is basically just a straight line from one end to the other. However, theres a ventilation system that runs parralel to the rest of the map for the most part, and its here that strategy comes into play. The vents give the Counter-Terrorists the chance to get to the hostages without having to go the more open, obvious and ultimately more dangerous main path. It also gives the Terrorists the oppurtunity to approach the CTs from the side and from behind as the move along the corridor. It basically comes down to the team that doesn’t use the shaft, loses.

The gameplay is simple, fun, yet annoyingly limited. The more I played, the more I could see a pattern-forming round after round. People would go through the vents, and if they met the other team – which they always would – they would either kill or be killed. If the former happened, they would get through, save the hostages, have a party. If they got killed, the remaining Counter-Terrorists outside the vent have the tricky and damn near impossible job of fighting people leaving the numerous grate exits and the other people coming down the main path. The vents small, enclosed space leaves little room for imagination, skill, or varying playing techniques – which is the main problem here.

The texture set used is a mixture of the Half-Life standard and others created especially for the map. The textures are used well, and create an original atmosphere and look. It all has a grimy industrial feel to it that is backed up by the lighting and architecture. Both of which are put together nicely. Sounds were used throughout the map, with you always being able to hear something going on. However, this had a reverse effect sometimes, as I just got so sick of the humming engines and computers, not to mention the large doors everytime they shut. In fact, these annoyed me so much I had to use a little *coughcheattypecommandthingtostopallambientnoises-veryusefullforhearingtheenemycough*.

FINAL WORD
Fun and simple is a good thing, but this map gets old fast due to a lack space for peoples varying playing styles.

RATING: 6.7
visual: 7
ambient: 7
gameplay: 6
All ratings out of 10.0
