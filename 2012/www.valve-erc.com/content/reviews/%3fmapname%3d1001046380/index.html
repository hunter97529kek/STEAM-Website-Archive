Hour-Glass
by Grey Dog
review by Jay 'Zaphod' Gill

INFORMATION
gametype: HL Single Player
number of players: 1
download: no local download available

First, before anything else is said, let me make this clear:

This map pack is inappropriate for younger players

As an introduction, here is an excerpt from the included text file:

"After turning down the Gman's offer of employment, you managed to escape using an intergalactic teleporter that returned you to Earth. Hiding out within the inner city, you soon believe that the Gman is no longer interested in you. A calm peaceful feeling begins to replace the fear, terror and horror that overwhelmed you during your nightmare with Black Mesa, the soldiers and the Xen species. Feeling confident that it is now safe to return home to your wife and look up some old friends, you make your way back to the old neighbourhood."

This sets up the start of your new mission. You begin in your comfy bedroom, and through trial-and-error, checking the various doors, you find a transporter tube. Eventually (hopefully!), you figure out that you need to locate the keycard to get out. Keycards, as well as other special items, will need to be collected throughout the missions (for example, in one section, you need to find a fuse to install in an electrical panel). There are on-screen prompts when you need a certain item.

Fairly soon, your missions will carry you into what I would call "the sleazy underbelly of wherever it is that these missions take place". In your quest as a "Time-Space Enforcement Officer", you find yourself in, of all places, a sleezy peep-show bar with nudie pics all over the place. This may be an appropriate place to say this again:

THIS MAP PACK IS INAPPROPRIATE FOR YOUNGER PLAYERS!

It's not only the static visuals, but scripted models and sounds that give this one an "NC-17" rating. I personally don't find that the adult-oriented content in this map pack adds anything to the game, but I'm sure there are those out there who like that stuff. (The author does give fair warning in the game and in the attached text file that there is explicit content)

The overall flow of the game is not bad, but there were several instances where I had to ask myself, "now what I am I doing here? What should I be after?" There are some limited scripted sequences with scientists or barneys that attempt to get a storyline going, but overall it didn't seem to gel. The bad-guy placement is well thought-out and you will be in for some very challenging fights, but the placement of those encounters is sporadic. There are several auto-save areas throughout the missions - and I suggest manually saving often.

Some new weaponry is available to you in this game, like a replacement for the hornetgun (functionally the same, with different gun model and ammo display), a sniper rifle like the one in OpFor, and a weapon that looks just like the hyperblaster from Quake 2. The 9mmAR weapon model is also replaced (though it functions just the same). Weapon and ammo placement is good, as well as health and HEV charge-ups. The author suggests using the scientists healing ability as well, and I certainly took advantage of that in several spots.

Generally, the architecture of the maps was unimpressive, but functional. I did notice a couple of areas where you could literally "get stuck" by jumping into an area that you could not get out of again, but all in all the world around you will not get in your way too much. The sequences where you ride on a trackcar are pretty bland, consisting of going in a part-circular path, or a spiral, and ending at a crossgate. In Half-Life, the trackcar sequences were all very exciting... you were changing elevation, fighting creatures or grunts, and shooting out pesky tripmines; also, there were sequences where the car would jump the track and wreck, or eject you into the slime. I think that just riding around a bit and stopping doesn't cut it - there was never anything to "do", so those parts should have been eliminated. Lighting is poor in some spots, but here again, the readme file that comes with the map pack states that this is the case, suggesting that you adjust your gamma settings if you find it too objectionable. Fans of Quake 2 will also quickly recognize that one of the map areas is almost a bare copy of one of the Quake 2 maps (I think it was the pumping station area). There is a good variety of puzzles, although some of them are rather obscure.

There are some new textures (some good, some not-so-good) and a few new models, some of which are shown in the screenshots. There is a goofy maintenance-type guy that walks around "doing things", and a chubby guy with a surpised look on his face and permanently-extended right hand that hangs out in the pervert bar, but most of the rest of the characters are the standard fare. You also get treated to a visit by your old buddy, the mysterious man in the blue suit. The sky textures are top-notch, and you can tell that a lot of time went into setting up the scripted sequences. Also, there are several "transition sequences" in the game, some that are sleazy and some that are downright wonderful (like the one that shows you approaching Earth from outer space - awesome!)

FINAL WORD
If you can get past the raunchy content, there is some decent gameplay here, but overall I found the map architecture to be bland. I have tried to separate the "adult content" from the gameplay elements as much as possible when scoring. As you will notice, there is no link given to the map file, for 2 reasons:

1: The author specifically asks that it not be done
2: The content of the map is not appropriate for everyone

Also, the link to the author's website did not work. So, if you want to see this map pack, you will have to find it on your own!

RATING: 6.0
visual: 6
ambient: 5
gameplay: 7
All ratings out of 10.0
