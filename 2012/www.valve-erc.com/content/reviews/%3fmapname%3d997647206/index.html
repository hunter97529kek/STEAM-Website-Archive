Lambda_Castle
by Piotr Lipinski
review by Chris 'Jabyaeye' Roys

INFORMATION
gametype: HLDM
number of players: 2-6
download: lambda_castle.zip

PiotErr has produced a map that has an unquivering Quake theme to it. The simplicity of the map is also the beauty of it. The maze of tiered hallways meander throughout the level allowing for players to frag to their heart's content.

The architecture found in lambda_castle is straight forward and lends itself to the maze of stone and wood that can be seen inside it. The arches are placed in such a way to replicate the jagged stones of real-life castles. As for the textures, they adhere to the castle theme very well. Their appearance belongs exclusively to that of a rough hewn building. This in turn enhances the beauty and mystique of playing in a castle. The textures could have changed up in places so as not to fall into the pit of rundundancy.

Atmosphere plays a large part in the creation of any map. The lighting in this map greatly enhances the uniqueness of it. The shadows that are cast by the torches leave an eery and spooky feeling. The ambient sounds are those of the Half-Life teleporter, which doubles as mood lighting. Other than the opening and closing of an occasional door, the only other sounds were some crickets by the outside bulwarks.

Gameplay was varied, and mostly spent on trying to figure out the current position of the map. The sameness of all the hallways led to player confusion if they wanted to return to some particular position on the map. The maze that ensues is a tangling network of redundant hallways that seems bent on confusing the player as to his whereabouts.

FINAL WORD
An interesting deathmatch map that provides an authentic castle feel, but is inhibited from great heights of stardom because of the hallway sameness and texture redundancy. This map, if changed a little, would be enhance its fun factor.

RATING: 6.0
visual: 6
ambient: 5
gameplay: 7
All ratings out of 10.0
