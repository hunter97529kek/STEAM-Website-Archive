Anarchy
by Gollum
review by Nic 'hads' McKay

INFORMATION
gametype: HLDM
number of players: 2-5
download: gmdm1.zip

It has been said by people far wiser than myself that first impressions are the most important. The first impression you get of any map is from the accompanying documentation, and sadly in this maps case that impression is, frankly, awful. Doing away with the standard readme.txt the author has decided to include a collection of HTML pages and images. After going through the fiddle of extracting them all from the zip file and opening the index page, any kind feelings I had quickly disappeared as I realised that, in layman’s terms, the page was broken.

However, Christmas good will saw me through getting my hands dirty fixing the HTML and I finally loaded up the map. Anarchy is the authors first released map and, sadly, it shows. The architecture is for the most part plain and boxy with uninspired texturing. Although for the most part the textures are neatly aligned and fairly appropriate, occasionally the choice of textures was questionable, such as the plain metal floor and awkward looking roof in the two main areas. There are some details to break up the square corridors, a vertical water pipe, a computer screen and some trims, and where the effort was made to include these it makes all the difference. The design in the underwater area is worth noting for its good use of details and interesting construction.

Equally, some effort has been made to give the map a atmosphere. I counted two ambient sounds, a bleeping computer and some drips, and while they are slightly illogical and far from consistent they do go some way to improving the overall experience of the map. However, the lighting does not. Throughout the map much more could have been made of the lighting to add atmosphere and variation. Particularly disappointing was the use of plain square lights on the roof of the main area which look awfully out of place.

So far we have a pretty typical first release. But the main thing that struck me about Anarchy was the large number of what, for lack of a better description, are design oddities. Although some of them can be put down to lack of experience, such as grossly over scaled signs and door frames filled with glass for no apparent reason, some aspects are just bizarre. Throughout the map staircases and other vertical aspects are initially filled up with breakable wood, which of course once walked on doesn’t respawn. The kindly female voice announces every time you pick up a weapon or item. There is a elevator between levels in the middle of a corridor. I am not sure if these things are a result of the author experimenting or just being perverse, but its all but impossible to pass judgement on them for while they are all technically near flawless they barely affect gameplay or atmosphere. Suffice to say that some may find these aspects of the map irritating, confusing or amusing .

The layout is certainly the maps strongest point. The two main areas are well connected by a warren of corridors and a underwater route. All the weapons are available and with a abundance of ammunition the gameplay tends to be very fast paced and for the most part fairly entertaining, although battles in the corridors are hampered by a distinct lack of space to move around. Testing should have resulted in these corridors being widened. The only other flaw in the layout comes from the health and suit chargers. These are in a rather sheltered corner of the map which results in a wounded player being able to almost entirely heal themselves before anyone else gets to them, a problem highlighted by the small number of players the map supports.

FINAL WORD
Overall Anarchy is a certainly competent first release which proves the authors technical ability with the tools. Hopefully the experience gained from this map will help the author iron out the design problems to combine improved architecture and atmosphere with the fun layout present here to make their next release a far superior one. Oddly, I couldn’t find a single joke to make in this review which makes for slightly boring reading and a slightly frustrated reviewer. If in doubt about your map include some comedy monkeys, that’s what I say.

RATING: 5.0
visual: 4
ambient: 5
gameplay: 6
All ratings out of 10.0
