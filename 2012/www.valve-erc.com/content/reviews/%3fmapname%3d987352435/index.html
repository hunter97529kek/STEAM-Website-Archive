Spasm
by Warpig
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: HLDM
number of players: 6 - 14
download: spasm.zip

Spasm is the first HLDM map by its author, although Warpig has had one other release in the shape of a Quake map released 5 years ago. This time away from the scene has not made Warpig lose any talent as Spasm is an impressive creation.

The thing that struck me on the first run around the map was the shear size of it. Vertically and horizontally the map seemed to be one of the largest HLDM maps I’ve played. After playing it for the last few days I still don’t seem to have a grasp on where everything is, often getting lost and turned around. This has some effect on gameplay as it stops the flow of things with each twist and turn.

Apart from that, gameplay is one of the great parts to the map, as it should be with any release. Warpig has made use of the vertical axis, something which isn’t seen as much as I’d like. Being able to throw grenades and snarks at the people scurrying below you is a pleasure I don’t get to enjoy often enough.

Weapon placement is yet another of the nice parts to the map. As I said before, it’s hard to remember where specific weapons are, but this doesn’t mean that it’s hard to find any weapon. There’s never one far away and the more powerful weapons such as the egon are in a suitably difficult place. Requiring a button press and a timely climb the appearing stairs just to get your hands on it.

R_speeds are one of the most important parts of a map; often people forget the limits of the engine and peoples computers when trying to make what they envisioned. Thankfully the r_speeds were reasonable. As said in the read-me they tend to peak in certain places but only in low traffic areas.

Ambience is often over-looked in maps, especially in the way of sounds. Such a small handful of maps have them that it’s rarely worth mentioning. Correct me if I’m wrong though, but in one of the grassy outdoor areas I do believe that there is a sound of a bird chirping. It added to the experience and was well suited to the area I was in -- extra points for that. The other part of ambience is of course lighting. It never really became a huge part of this map. Much of the map is outdoors meaning shadows are unobtrusive. The parts that are indoors are well lit and there is never an area where it’s difficult to see. One plus point is that every light has a source, which is something I like to see. Coloured light makes a small appearance in a passage at the very centre of the map, but due to the small area in which it is used it isn’t an issue.

Last but not least comes graphics. The textures were nicely put together and I never noticed one out of place or misaligned. However, it is architecture that really shines. Every door has an arch, if corner has a curve and one of the courtyards is really quite impressive. I feel that gameplay is the most important part of a map, but the truly great ones must have looks as well.

FINAL WORD
For an authors first release in 5 years, this is very impressive. Gameplay and graphics make for a map you should definitely own.

RATING: 7.3
visual: 8
ambient: 7
gameplay: 7
All ratings out of 10.0
