Purgatory
by Fletch
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: HLDM
number of players: 2 - 4
download: purgatory.zip

Much like the last map I reviewed, this one takes a lot of inspiration from Quake, or at least it appears that way. The jump pads that are used to get around are obviously similar to those used in Quake 3, and the small layout perfected for 1 on 1 is much like many of the custom maps in the Quake scene.

Gameplay is great in so many ways in this level. Best played with just 2 players LAN play is optimised, but this map can be fun also with up to 4 players. Connectivity and the layout make the gameplay fast with no room for campers, although due to its simplicity things may get boring after a while. Weapon placement as a whole was good, although the choice to include the Egon was a bad one. Due to its size and the easiness to get this gun its far to easy to own the map with it. It would have been better to have included the RPG or Gauss in the position of the Egon.

Architecture is superb in this map, with time obviously taken to make the giant hand protruding from the wall. Curved surfaces were used wisely, while never creating problems with r_speeds. Textures were a mix of some excellently made MikeZilla custom ones and the original HL included wads. They were put to use in an imaginative and creative way, and created a unique feel through out the level.

Ambience in the map was the same as with most DM levels, lighting didn’t distract from the gameplay thankfully but wasn’t very inventive or interesting either. A plus however is that every light had a source, something which isn’t usually done in DM maps. Sounds weren’t used at all, which is a shame as they can greatly add to a maps ambience and mood.

FINAL WORD
This is classic DM and a classic map. Small, simple, fun. What more could you ask for?

RATING: 7.0
visual: 7
ambient: 6
gameplay: 8
All ratings out of 10.0
