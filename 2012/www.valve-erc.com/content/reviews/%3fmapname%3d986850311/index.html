HQ Desert
by Roger Wilkinson
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: Half-Life Deathmatch
number of players: 4 to 10
download: rogdm2.zip

HQ Desert is the second map by author Roger Wilkinson. His first didn’t score too well due to its many bugs and obscene r_speeds. Lets hope that his second attempt is better than his first.

The map is large and open with facing buildings on either side. Texturing is not flawed but is boring and dull. Something a bit brighter or some nice lighting effects would have been nice to liven it up.

Architecture is inconsistent. There are times when you think it is poor and all the rooms look the same, but there are touches everywhere which look nice. A curved endcap, a bridge with little pointy bits. It adds something to the map which makes it more enjoyable.

Sounds are something which I don’t bother writing about usually. So few maps actually make use of them, it seems unfair to criticise a map for not having them. So I won’t and simply say that there are none.

Weapon placement is good for the most part. They are well spread out and the nice connectivity means you should be able to find a gun no problem. One quibble is that in the centre of the map there is an Machine gun suspended on a rock. Being in such a dangerous place it would have been nice to have one of the more powerful weapons there. An RPG would have been ideal.

Unlike his last map which seemed specially designed for sniping, this map has been left open for any number of styles. Staying inside will insure close combat and the chance of rooftop sniping. Staying outside, while leaving yourself open for attack will bring you more long distance fighting.

FINAL WORD
Although not genius, the map does bring some fun with it. Architecture is decent but texturing let it down. It’s a step up from his last map, but there’s a long way to go yet.

RATING: 6.0
visual: 6
ambient: 6
gameplay: 6
All ratings out of 10.0
