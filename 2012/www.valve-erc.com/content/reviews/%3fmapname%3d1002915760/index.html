Mausoleum
by JUIM
review by Nic 'hads' McKay

INFORMATION
gametype: HLDM
number of players: 10 - 30
download: MAUSOLEUM.zip

My reaction when I loaded up this map for the first time about two weeks ago was along the lines of "I should go to bed". After a good few good nights sleep I loaded it up again. It wasn’t something in my drink. It wasn’t some bizarre flash back of a childhood, experience. It was real. And so I start this review by warning you that this map is nothing like anything I have ever seen during the waking hours of my life before.

According to the readme this map is supposed to be set in a film set. After getting used to the whole concept, aside from the fact that the only films I could imagine being filmed here would be banned for encouraging the abuse of narcotics, its visually quite attractive. The inside of the map is really divided into two distinct areas, the first of which consists of large marble rooms donned with flicking pink lanterns, giant eye balls and large crucifixes. The second area is more spacious and filled with odd aqueduct type architecture, but still lit by flickering lanterns. Considering how original this all is, the author has done an impressive job overall, helped by a few nice touches such as broken support beams and spray coming off the waterfall.

There are, however, a few problems. The constant flickering lights become irritating and there are times when there are lights without a visible source. Flicking colored lights are a special effect, and any good film maker will tell you that special effects are only special if their not overused. At times the texturing seems a little random, such as brick texture suddenly being used on walls. A bit more effort is needed here to make the texturing consistent. The outside area was disappointing, with bland lighting and even blander lighting. Considering a night sky was used it seems odd that the whole outside area should be brightly lit. There was also a dearth of sounds in the map, surprising given considering the obvious thought given to the other aspects on ambience.

Sadly, this map is crippled by an ill thought out layout. The two main areas of the map are all but inaccessible to each other. The only way between the two areas of the map were either by a secret passage or by using a one-way teleporter. With a small group of players this absolutely chokes the gameplay, as it is possible to be all but stuck in one area with nothing to do except aimlessly try to puzzle out the cryptic layout of the map. This is not helped by doors which for some random reason need to be "used" to be opened, and areas which simply lead to dead ends or hidden passages. That said, when you do manage to get to one of the several arenas the combat is good fun, much helped by the excellent use of vertical space throughout.

The weapon layout also suffers from secret passage syndrome. There are painfully few weapons in some areas until you figure out you can "use" various signs and symbols around the map to open up secret areas. This reliance on knowing the secrets not only drastically slows the pace of gameplay, but also makes the map all but useless for a group of players wanting to give the map a quick try. A few secret areas add a lot to be map but relying on them for the structure of the map is a mistake. All the weapons are available apart from the egon, although with the huge number of secret areas its perfectly possible all the weapons are in the map and I simply failed to find them. Spawn location needed a bit more thought to help prevent players spawning in the middle of fire-fight. You can even spawn on the roof occasionally, which is crazy as you can jump into the sky.

FINAL WORD
Its clear a lot of time and effort has been put into this map and it is in many ways a very impressive work. Sadly, it is spoiled by a poor layout and reliance on secret areas. Hopefully the author will be able to fix these issues and continue to offer a unique visual treat in his next release.

RATING: 5.7
visual: 6
ambient: 7
gameplay: 4
All ratings out of 10.0
