Duel Arena
by Piotr Lipinski
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: HLDM
number of players: 2 - 8
download: pldm_duel01.zip

You appear in a large room and gaze up, noticing the glorious shining stars you lose yourself for a minute. Dreaming for a life you could have had, if only things had turned out differently. Then some guy throws a grenade at you and your dead.

Not exactly how you feel when entering the map, but I have a fetish for the dramatic. The first thing I did notice when spawning in was the great architecture. The large main room has detailed walls that are anything but flat. Looking further up will reveal some nice girders and the stars beyond. The architecture isn’t consistent throughout the map, however. Some of the connecting corridors were either plain or or just ugly. R_speeds weren’t too much of a problem for me, but others may have some troubles as they get a little high in places. Texturing is done in a brown industrial motif and is varied, showing that the author has taken time to choose the right texture for each brush.

Gameplay is a shining example of how a DM map should be made. The map is fast and furious and due to its numerous and easily accesible floors action is never far away, even with only two players. This means it’s a great LAN map, but is large enough to still be entertaining with 10 players. Fights are mixed between close range shotgun-ing fun in the passage ways and vertical long distance wars. The variety means that there is plenty to keep you coming back for more, you shouldn’t get bored easily with this one.

Ambience is the only down side really to this map. New sounds are non-existent to coincide with almost every map in existence. The only sound in it at all was a siren in the main room which would continuously drone on for all eternity to the point where I would rather type ‘/kill’ in the console than play. Lighting was basic, coloured lighting wasn’t used and there were no dark corners or any special lighting effects. Having said this, DM maps aren’t the greatest for their ambience as it can tend to detract from the gameplay.

The faults of the map lie within the weapon choice. Playing it with a small number of people I found it far to easy to dominate with the Hornet Gun, it would have been a good idea to leave it out. The other gun that seemed slightly out of place at times was the RPG, but this is just a minor quibble.

FINAL WORD
Funky looking visuals and fun gameplay mean this is a great map. Ambience and weapon choice lets down the side a little, but this shouldn’t stop you picking up and playing.

RATING: 7.0
visual: 8
ambient: 6
gameplay: 7
All ratings out of 10.0
