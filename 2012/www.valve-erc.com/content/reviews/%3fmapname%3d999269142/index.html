Samurai
by Matt Finitz
review by Artbroken

INFORMATION
gametype: TFC Territorial Control Variant
number of players: 12 - 32
download: samurai.zip

The search for new and interesting maps is a sort of pilgrimage; a journey of sifting through websites and magazines, checking out the chatrooms and the messageboards, for any news of the thing which often eludes us: New, unique, and well constructed maps to play.

I confess, games like Shadow Warrior and Daikatana had given me preconceived notions of what a map named "Samurai" might be like. I imagined big burly characters in flowing robes with wild hair and large curved-blade swords running about. I began to let go of these notions as a I fired up the map, and spawned into a room with rice paper windows, with the sound of a waterfall echoing, and the sky visible through a cracked stone-slab roof.

And then, I stepped outside.......

I found myself high up in the peaks of a dormant volcanic island, surrounded by water and the towering cliffs of the mainland. The sky was a morning mist, and the grass beneath my feet seemed to glisten with dew. High rock walls sprinkled with fauna formed a rough circle from where I stood, and in the center of it, a large mound protruded up to form a small plateau. I climbed up to the top via ropes hung off the sides, and from there I could see four passages branching off of this central "hub", each marked with it's own peculiar symbol: Wolf, Crab, Dragon, and Mantis. While pondering these, I happened to look down, and saw an opening into the mound below me. Although too small to fit through, I could see that the mound was indeed hollow, and that inside was a sort of shrine with these same four symbols, along with a fifth symbol: The mysterious and rare symbol of the Samurai. At the periphery of the room, I could just glimpse what appeared to be water passages branching off in the general direction of the above ground tunnels. Curiousity piqued, I hopped down off of the mound and began to explore.....

The map is divided into six major areas: The central hub where teams first spawn, the four "animal spirit" areas, and the Samurai spirit shrine. The cloverleaf layout suggests symmetry, yet each area boasts individual architecture that clearly sets each apart from the rest. The few textures that are used blend seamlessly with one another and the surrounding sky. I was quite surprised at how well the TFC grass texture complemented the custom rock textures. On the other hand, I was a bit dismayed to see the ubiquitous Half-Life water texture again. The huge waterfalls in the wolf area, at various points, can give the player a HOM effect, but you'd have to be looking for it. I could also nitpick about being able to reach out and touch the sky in places, but the view is so nice....

Since Samurai is nearly entirely an outdoor map, most of the light comes from ambient sources. Shadows appear where you would expect - not much more or less than that. There is a rather nice flickering effect in the underwater passages, which closely resembles reflected light passing through water. Also, in the Samurai shrine, light beams down through the opening overhead. Custom sounds make more of a difference. The old bleating of the Vox has been replaced by a feminine voice whose announcements are accompanied by an oriental-style musical flourish. Dripping water and waterfall sounds are also used where appropriate. A trail of sprite bubbles in the underwater chasm of the Wolf spirit area leads players to that area's capture point.

At first glance, Samurai appears to be a simple Territorial Control map. That would be a gross misstatement. While gameplay in Samurai has elements of that style, it is far removed from the gameplay in, say, Canalzone. A brief overview is in order.

The samurai sword appears when the teams first spawn. The player who takes it becomes that team's Samurai. As the sword is carried into other areas, the respawn points for that team will change to those same areas. Team members attempt to capture the four animal spirits by touching their respective capture points. Once all four are captured, the player with the samurai sword may then go and harvest the Samurai Spirit.

Gameplay is frenetic. Without standard bases to occupy, and not much in the way of cover anywhere in the map, expect the battle to seesaw many times before a victor emerges. The size and features of the different areas each lend themselves to different classes. The central hub will be a sure favorite for engineers and demomen. Snipers will find the open space and hiding spots of the Wolf and Crab areas appealing, but will probably stay clear of the small Dragon Spirit area, where HW guys and soldiers will more likely dominate. One issue to be aware of from the start is the opportunity for camping around the spawn points. If one team loses control of their samurai sword, they have no choice but to respawn wherever it last fell, and it can be easy to get pinned down from there. I also recommend larger groups of players to avoid the round turning into a circuitous footrace. This map is huge, and can easily accomodate a full 32 players (although the spawn points might get a bit crowded with that many).

FINAL WORD
Take this map and make a pilgrimage to your favorite server.

RATING: 8.7
visual: 9
ambient: 8
gameplay: 9
All ratings out of 10.0
