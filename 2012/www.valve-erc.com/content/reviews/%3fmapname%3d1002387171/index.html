Huey
by Pimperator
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: HLDM
number of players: 3 - 8
download: hldm_huey.zip

Nice. Nice. Say it with me. Nice. Isn’t that just the worst possible word to express your approval of something? There is no weaker more pathetic superlative I can think of, and yet it’s probably the most commonly used. Does this mean we’re all pathetic with a complete lack of literary skills? No, not really. It just means that someone needed an introduction to a review, and couldn’t think of anything good. Again.

Huey is the second map to be released by it’s author, and the second to be reviewed here at the VERC. You’d be mistaken however, to think that the first to be reviewed was his first released. No, in the spirit of doing things differently, the review of his third map as written by hads can be read here.

Huey’s gameplay is one of those things that however much you think about it, you just can’t decide exactly what you think. On one hand it’s frag-fest, atrium based fun… for the most part. Then on the other hand, it has connectivity problems which greatly hurt the pace. It has this feel to it which makes me think that the author was just making it up as he went along. He created an arena to fight in, then created another two. When trying to connect them however, all he’s done is put passages between and effectively made circle. The only thing that stops this from being exactly the case is the addition of a teleporter, and even this didn’t make much of a difference. It feels like there was no plan before hand, the layout and design was not planned but merely made up as the map was put together. Maybe I’m right, and most likely I’m wrong, but just the fact it feels this way shows that there’s something not quite right with the design of this map.

Although the connectivity problems hamper the gameplay a great deal there are other parts of it that help the author redeem himself. Item placement is good, with there always being a gun handy. This made the action very fast and very furious. The author has also wisely chosen which weapons to be used, as none of them allow you to own completely. Unless your me of course, where l337 skills by far make up for the lack of egon. Have I an ego? Nah, I’m way too cool for that.

The aesthetics of the map are a somewhat grey affair as well, neither astoundingly inspired nor scarily dodgy. Their consistent, stable and all round decent showing an effort to make the map look good, and succeeding in many areas. Some of the connecting passages are very well designed and the architecture impressive, but some of the atriums could have been spiced up that little bit more. The problem is mainly the lack of detail, in all of the different departments. Architecture is sound, with rounded corners but nothing particularly interesting. Texturing is consistent, and the ones used go together well. But there isn’t enough variation in colours.

Colour is not a problem however when it comes to the lighting. Now I know that coloured lighting can make you feel a little bit queasy, but it’s been used fantastically well in this map. The colours are pale enough not to become disgusting, but are still used often enough that it makes the map look a whole lot more interesting. I really don’t have a bad word to say about this area of the map, which is an unusual thing. Sounds are a different matter however. Many mappers forget this aspect of mapping, and how much they can add to the immersion in a map.

There are however, other features in this map to make it a bit more special from your regular run of the day stuff. The sky is particularly nice, as clouds sail above. There’s a cunningly placed trap at the RPG, which I’ll let you find out about for yourself and there’s a nice Pimperator sign with custom sprites. These things while small, add something to the map that make you remember it just that little bit more.

FINAL WORD
Huey is neither bad or fantastic. It’s in that area somewhere in between, a fair distance above average but with not enough things to make the map great or more interesting, it’s simply… nice.

RATING: 7.0
visual: 7
ambient: 7
gameplay: 7
All ratings out of 10.0
