Sparta
by Schrade
review by Artbroken

INFORMATION
gametype: HLDM
number of players: 8 - 14
download: sparta.zip

Schrade has chosen a rather interesting - and fitting - name for this map. The word "Sparta" conjures up all sorts of imagery. It is, most famously, the name of a city in Greece. A city once renowned for the prowess of its warriors and home to some key figures of ancient mythology. Two such figures were Castor and Polydeuces, the Hero Twins, for whom the Gemini constellation is named. "Though twins, only Polydeuces was considered godly enough to be admitted to heavenly Olympus, while his brother was dispatched to the Underworld as a mere mortal. However, Polydeuces interceded on his twin's behalf, on the plea that he could not bear eternal separation. The gods relented to the extent that the two were allowed to remain together forever, spending half the year deep in the earth beneath their shrine in Sparta and the other half on Olympus."

I think the twins might recognize some resemblance in Schrade's handiwork. On the one side of Sparta, you'll find an Olympus-like courtyard, featuring an arched spire over water, surrounded by rock wall three tiers high. At the other end, an underworldly grotto lit by torches, with a sacrificial altar jutting out over an abyss. The courtyard and abyss areas are connected via passageways which lead from three large rooms, which house some decidedly un-ancient technology.

The architecture and texturing in the map are top notch, led by the overworld/underworld areas. The central rooms are a bit boxy, but the size and detail of the rooms helps alleviate any sensation of deathmatch-in-a-cube. Some flaws exist, however. In the courtyard area there are several places where the player can run up against the sky. Although I've dismissed this issue in some maps, in this particular map I feel it detracts, because it isn't handled with consistency. It gives the sense that this area was left unpolished. The other flaws in this area are places where one can see behind the architecture, as if you were flying around outside of the map in no-clip mode. This is a big visual blunder, and drops an otherwise great looking map down from the level of a professional quality release.

Speaking of the sky, a nice custom sky would have really added to this map. As it is, the default desert sky adds nothing to the ambience suggested by the architecture. The outdoor lighting is plain, not bright enough for full afternoon sun, not dark or reddish enough to be twilight. The indoor areas are lit with a combination of torches and tiny fixtures, never varying in intensity or hue. The torches are rather nice, and the scorch marks behind and above them nicer still. What's missing is a nice crackling sound of burning wood, which would have complemented the sound of swirling wind in the courtyard. The indoor passages and rooms, with their higher technology motif, could also have used some spicing up. The pipes and wiring leave plenty of opportunity for creative touches, such as sparks, beams, and lasers. There's also no noise from the pipes, no electrical hum, no whine of machinery.

What this map does have going for it is a nicely arranged flow of movement. From the courtyard, all paths will naturally lead one to the abyss area. Jumping into the abyss results in being teleported back to the courtyard area, with the sound of your own screams in your ears. While this teleport system is a bit novel, and perhaps controversial (Jumping into the sky, and then falling down from it?), it does serve to keep players moving throughout the map. And you will want to keep moving. If the ancient Spartans had an arsenal like this, we'd all be speaking greek. Look here: Three crossbows; two RPG's, shotguns, and Mp-5's; a gauss gun, a gluon gon, and even a hornet gun, AND all the tripmines, detpacks, and hand grenades you can carry. Oh, and snarks too. (What? No .357? Sadly, no.) There's even ammo for your base pistol laying around, for crying out loud.

Snipers will have an absolute field day with this map, given the layout. At either end, waiting for players to emerge from the central passages is a no-brainer. Ditto for the RPG. Players with mere shotguns and assault rifles would be better served sticking indoors and lying in wait with satchel charges and tripmines. The only saving grace outdoors is the bounce pads, which play havoc with the RPG'S and keep the snipers off balance. Snarks could be fun too, if you live long enough to use them: They are only found on the way down in the abyss.

FINAL WORD
Like our Hero Twins, Sparta aspires towards Godly excellence, but ultimately remains somewhat in the underworld. Schrade definitely has some chops in the architecture department, but a little more polish and detail would have pushed this map into rarer air. Likewise, while the bounce pads and teleporter help the flow of the map, in the end it's a blitzkrieg. Not that that isn't any fun, or anything...

RATING: 7.0
visual: 8
ambient: 6
gameplay: 7
All ratings out of 10.0
