Synthetic Arena
by Dan Houser 82
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: TFC
number of players: 2 - 8
download: synth1.exe

Let it be known I’ve never been a huge TFC fan. I play it occasionally I admit but I’m afraid I’ve never been drawn to it’s watery shores. You have been warned. Now on with the show.

The map Synthetic Arena was described by its author as having “Just enough room for strategy”. Now, considering the map is a small arena with 4 rooms for team spawn points I’m not sure there’s much room for anything. I played it with 4 people, 1 person in each team and the gameplay was chaotic to say the least. With the gametype chosen and the layout of the map any chances for defence are immediately thrown out the window. The open, small, one levelled arena also deletes any point for a sniper class. Now this can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand no one likes being repeatedly killed by a sniper, on the other hand you’ve just alienated the people that like being snipers.

Architecture isn’t a predominant part of such a small map. The spawn points were basically square from the inside with another brush in the corner to make it ‘interesting’ in the loosest meaning of the word. Four pillars surrounded the centre of the map and…that’s about it. Texturing was the one point of the map that stood out, apart from the loud colours with a sort of medieval motif looked really good. Which is something quite strange as you wouldn’t think that medieval loud reds would ever look good.

Ambience is non-existent. The map is magically lit from invisible bulbs or something, I’m not sure. and there are no sounds in there either. As you would expect from such a small map the r_speeds max out at about 220, you should have no trouble playing the map then. Just before I leave you I’ll point out another flaw. There is nothing stopping you from entering the oppositions spawn room. This means that the only strategy ever needed in this map is one person to sit in the centre racking up the points every few seconds and then one other person sitting in the spawn blowing the crap out of anything that appears, not exactly fun but you can bet someone will do it.

FINAL WORD
I can see what the author was going for with gameplay, but from what I witnessed it never really worked. A good idea for a gametype was crippled by design flaws.

RATING: 4.3
visual: 5
ambient: 4
gameplay: 4
All ratings out of 10.0
