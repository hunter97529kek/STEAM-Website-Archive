CS_Altosierra
by [fCo]CaptSensible
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: CS Hostage Rescue
number of players: 6 - 14
download: cs_altosierra.zip

The gameplay in the map is one of the plus points, and reminds me of the old days of CS. The numerous passageways leading along ledges and through tunnels allows for some sneaky stealth kills. Players can jump out from any corner, keeping you on your toes. The action isn’t fast like some CS maps, but more based around strategy. Biding your time usually pays off more than rushing in.

Due to the positioning of the hostages they aren’t really used so much. The best thing for the CT’s to do is position themselves in the nooks of cliffs and await the terrorists coming down. Then they can get the spring on them. Another thing is the difficulty of defending the hostages, in an area where the only one way to go. Due to the many twists and turns the map could be quite difficult to learn, but it is reasonably small so you should be fine after 3 or 4 rounds.

The reasons I’ve mentioned for things I liked about the map could be the reasons others don’t. The gameplay won’t be suited to everyone’s tastes, especially the new wave of CS players who never saw the earlier betas. If you like rushing in and fighting a round in 45 seconds, then you can do it in this map. But you’ll be missing its charm, and no doubt be bored after a few rounds. Play as a team and it’s a whole different game. It’s size and layout make it the thinking mans map, and thinking is what CS should be about.

Visually the map is very impressive. The waterfall looks great, the tree is one of the best I’ve seen in the HL engine, or at least from certain angles, and the architecture on the cliffs must have taken a long time to do. It doesn’t look man made, as it shouldn’t, although there is the downside that it’s very easy to get stuck in the rock formations. The textures used were from the map as_tundra, and they fitted this map nicely.

Ambience in the map is achieved mostly through the textures and architecture, they make you feel as if you are in the mountains. Lighting was barely used due to the map being set outside. Sound helped due to its use in the waterfall, which would have felt odd had it not been there.

FINAL WORD
Maybe not everyone’s cup of tea, but definitely worth a try just in case it is.

RATING: 7.3
visual: 8
ambient: 7
gameplay: 7
All ratings out of 10.0
