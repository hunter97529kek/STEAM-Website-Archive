De_Gypt
by Mazy
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: CS Bomb Defuse
number of players: 6 - 12
download: de_egypt.zip

As I sit in my laz-e-boy leather reclining seat, in front of my top of the line PC,1.8Ghz P4, 512meg o’ ram, 21” monitor, 40gig hard drive, I lean back, open my eyes and think “Hold on a minute!”. The back of the seat I’m sitting on is broken, and only reclines cause its hanging off, and my PC isn’t that good at all! Plus, where did all those women go?

I hate it when you imagine things to be better than they really are, only to find that your life is considerably less perfect. One of the good things about maps is that you get what you see, and theres no fooling involved. And when playing this map, you’ll see it’s good. Really quite good indeed.

I would normally mention Gameplay first, as if a map plays bad no amount of visual kebabs can make it any better. But with this map I’ll make an exception, simply because it looks cool. Not just normal cool either, that sort of funky kewl. The textures are custom, which adds a much needed freshness to the map, something that would be missing if it used normal textures. Everything fits together quite well, and although it doesn’t look much like the Egypt I recently visited, its impressive none the less. Architecture is also very impressive, without ever being obtrusive. It's detailed enough also so you never feel the map is boxy. There is also a rather impressive looking palm tree, created by Telnets.

Gameplay is nothing to be frowned upon either. Things didn’t seem to biased, and maybe it was just my fantastic skill balancing things out but I didn’t feel hampered or disadvantaged/advantaged because of the layout of the map. Connectivity was good, and things fitted together well. The areas Mazy has created always provide a fun and interesting battle, never stale, and never predictable. Your bound to have fun on this one.

Ambience is one of the shining parts of the map (and believe me, there are many). Although as I said, it looks nothing like the Egypt I visited, you get the feeling your in a hot, Arabic building. The textures and lighting create a mood not often seen. The lighting is one of the best things about the map, shadows are cast beautifully and it looks great. Sounds were again disappointingly absent. Again this is a case of people over-looking them, but then again I can’t see anywhere where they might have been used. Maybe with some added running water in one of the small pools things would have been improved, or maybe some birds squawking in the wind. Ah well, you can’t have it all.

FINAL WORD
Wowific (c/o Delete_Me).

RATING: 8.3
visual: 9
ambient: 8
gameplay: 8
All ratings out of 10.0
