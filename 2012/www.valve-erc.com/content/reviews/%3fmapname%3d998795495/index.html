De_Torn, Part 1
by Crinity and Madcross
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: CS Bomb Defuse
number of players: 10 - 32
download: no local download available

The sun rises, and the beams shine down on the land below. The once bustling Turkish village is quiet now, silenced by the fires of war. Only the birds have returned, while the flames still burn, painting shadows on the walls and cobble streets where children once played. But now a new conflict is brewing as a small terrorist group see their chance and move in the town to poison the water supply and a Counter-Terrorist team is sent to stop them. War is in the air once more.

De_Torn is without a doubt the most hyped map Half-Life has seen. If you’ve been to any of the biggest Half-Life news sites you will have seen the screens, heard the words, and no doubt been impressed, for this is the finest looking map your ever likely to see in this engine. With completely new textures created by both Crinity and Madcross, the maps freshness, crispness, and fantastic detail set the atmosphere that backs up the well-thought out gameplay that can handle large numbers, public servers, and strategic clan matches.

You can tell after hours of playing this map that it’s been planned precisely, and nothing has been left to chance. This is mostly due to the timing of everything. Most of the Counter-Strike maps that are already in there have problems with one team being disadvantaged by the map, the other team takes the vantage point before you can get there. De_Torn is without such problems, as you arrive almost exactly at the same time as the other team, and this is without the map being symmetrical. The gameplay is also greatly improved due to the areas that have been created by Crinity and Madcross, things are varied, interesting, and things are always different.

This map also has the ability to stay fresh for a long time, this is due to the huge range of choices a player can take in each battle. There are a number of different routes from each area which mean there is a good amount of tactical twists and turns that can change the outcome of the round.

The one downside to gameplay however, is created because of the advantage just described. With the many choices of routes provided, things are prone to get confusing, new-comers to the map will be lost for a good while, and when people die and the numbers get small it can be a chore to find people. Because of this the map can get boring, and may kick itself in a teeth. The Counter-Strike community is well-known for it’s rush to judge, and when people play this map and spend a lot of time lost they may just disregard it and move on. Only time will tell if this will happen, but if it does it’ll be a crying shame.

Visually, the map is amazing. Textures, architecture, lighting, you can’t beat it. When CS_Italy got into Counter-Strike, people were impressed, and with good reason. It seemed crisp, new and different. De_Torn will give you the same feeling times a thousand. I can’t honestly see anything looking better than this in the Half-Life engine. It’s not that there’s one thing that sticks out in your mind as looking great, it’s that the architecture and textures paint a setting which is a pleasure to play in. The only thing wrong with the visuals, was one misaligned texture on a small piece of wall. Unfortunately, since that’s the only mistake I found, it doesn’t leave much for me to talk about.

Ambience is where most maps fall down, trying to use light and sound in a way that creates a mood and gives an impression of where you are is no easy task, but it has been done here to a point. For a lot of the time you're outside, meaning that the only light is from the sun. This can be a problem in some maps, as it’s not very interesting when everything is lit the same way. Fortunately however there are places in De_Torn where you venture indoors, and the rooms are lit with torches sitting on walls. These create shadows which make things look more real. Sounds are the other side to ambience, so commonly forgotten while they can add so much to a map. A set of sounds are provided in Torn, from wind rushing up the streets, the fire of guns in the distance, running water that’ll make you want to pee, and thunder rattling in your ears. They’re subtly added, but with an effect that makes you believe you're in this town, and not just at a computer.

The downsides to this map are small, and I have trouble thinking of any. If I really thought hard I could say the size of the map make games with a few people a bore, or that it would be nice if there was some more vertical gameplay. But if I was to say that, it would only be nit-picking.

FINAL WORD
The map is stunning, you can’t dispute that fact. The visuals create a beautiful world to play in, the ambience creates a setting and a mood that tells you where you are, and the gameplay is planned so that it’s never boring, and always fun.

RATING: 9.7
visual: 10
ambient: 10
gameplay: 9
All ratings out of 10.0
