Ao_estate
by -Tec-
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: CS Hostage Rescue
number of players: 6 - 10
download: ao_estate2.zip

The house scenario. Quite possibly the most over-used map scenario in CS. Apart from the original cs_mansion that featured in Beta 1 of CS there has been 4 other versions of mansion, a new official CS map called Estate and a sackful of custom maps. Ao_estate was created as a replacement for cs_estate, the reasonably popular CS map by Zaphod_Beeblebrox. The reason this map has the AO prefix rather than the standard CS is because it was made for the AO clan. With that explained, on with the review.

The biggest problem for the map is in the basic design of the house. The map consists of about 4 or 5 different areas. The street out the front where the CT’s spawn, the boiler room, the roof, and the area at the front door. The problems is, most of the action takes place in the same place, the front door. The CT’s come through the garden and approach the door. The Terrorists in the meantime have positioned themselves on the opposite side. The door opens, alerting everyone with the moaning creak, the Terrorists fire through the door killing the advancing CT’s. This is pretty much how the map played the entirety of my game. In total there were 4 entrances to the house. Apart from the front door you could approach through the swimming pool and into the kitchen, the boiler room that leads up from under the house and also to the kitchen. Meaning that two entrances are easily guarded and can be simply covered by the same people at the front door. As for the last entrance, this is a set of ladders at the side of the house leading to a vent or the roof. Whatever way you go it always results in the same thing, Terrorists win.

The faults in gameplay unfortunately don’t end there, the hostages for example. There’s one in the living room, one in the bedroom, and two more in the boiler room. Because they are all so far apart and some of them are on their own no one brings them into play. Again, also, making the map easier for terrorists.

Architecture was a better aspect of the map, although nothing amazing, it did feel very ‘house’. Simply the fact that the map is supposed to be a house deletes the possibility for any mucho exciting brushwork, but things were OK considering. Texturing was reasonable, recycling the standard wads for the most part. The outside of the house uses the cs_italy textures (something else which is overused, and features in this map). Texturing as a whole was good, with no blatant misalignments. Although there is one area where the flower wallpaper is overstretched making the place look ugly.

Lighting in the map was nothing special, but the outside of the house did look very nice in places. Sounds were used in a number of places throughout, the only one I really noticed however was in the boiler room. As a whole sounds added little to the map. Apart from the usual ways of ambience, sound and lighting, it was created another way. The living room, for example, had a TV, video recorder, DVD player and loads of films. This made the map more real and helped add to realism.

FINAL WORD
All in all the map wasn’t drastically flawed, but niggling problems in visuals and basic gameplay flaws mean that it adds nothing to a tired scenario. Stick with Zaphod’s estate, it has a funky doorbell.

RATING: 5.7
visual: 6
ambient: 6
gameplay: 5
All ratings out of 10.0
