CS_Subway
by Jabyaeye
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: CS Hostage Rescue
number of players: 5 vs 5
download: Cs_subway.zip

Many factors must be considered when making a map, and any released map should have all these factors included for a complete experience. Just look at our rating system, it includes the three basic points and to get a good score all three are required. The map must be visually appealing, textures and architecture should combine to make things more real. The desired effect should make you forget your surroundings, and be placed in the virtual world. Ambience must be there to help build emotion. Fear, tension, relief should all be built from whether your surroundings are dark, moody with subtle creepy noises or whether your outside in the sun with only trees and bird noises. Finally gameplay must be there to make the map fun, enjoyable and to make you want to keep playing. Only the truly brilliant maps manage to completely achieve these, and I could name maybe 2 or 3. Unfortunately this isn’t one of them.

Of course that’s not to say you should leave now, this is of course a beta map and the reason it was submitted was so the author could find what should be changed before the final release. As with most maps, there are good points. Gameplay for example is in places nothing but straight good fun, but things seemed a little biased against Terrorists and CT’s usually stormed it. The other main gripe is that the objective was completed only once in the entire time I played. To fix this I would recommend moving the lone hostage in the office in with the lone hostage in the train. This would mean that CT’s would have less trouble trying to get the shockingly bad AI to follow and be more willing to complete the map the way it was intended.

Sounds are a rare treat in HL maps but I said that with my last review and here I’m saying it again. There is one new sound in the map, a phone ringing. It doesn’t make a huge difference to ambience but it’s a nice touch. Lighting was a bit plain and there was some need for a darker moodier atmosphere. Being in a subway you would expect things to be run down and a broken or flickering light in places would have gone a long way.

The Visual aspect needs some changes. The problem is everything’s too clean, and it seems the vandals that did the graffiti have taken great care in making sure their designs covered exactly one section of wall. The people where I come from are completely careless and simply do their tags on any surface possible. As for the other textures, its just to plain. Add some detail and some grunge will make it more pleasing to the eye. Architecture could do with some work as well, many rooms are boring and seem very square. Add a pillar here, a curved arch here and keep an eye on r_speeds. Soon you’ll have a beautiful subway covered in graffiti, sick, and old homeless people to really add some authenticity.

FINAL WORD
Gameplay was the one redeeming if flawed feature in an otherwise lacklustre map, fix these up and submit again. You may have a winner.

RATING: 5.3
visual: 5
ambient: 5
gameplay: 6
All ratings out of 10.0
