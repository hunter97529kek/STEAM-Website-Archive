Castle Wars
by Spin_Oza
review by Graham 'Gonnas' Smith

INFORMATION
gametype: HLDM
number of players: 5 - 12
download: castle_warsv2.zip

Castle Wars is, as you guessed, set in a castle. The large map allows for multiple ways of killing and can be great fun to frag in. Both in looks and gameplay, this map impresses.

Gameplay is great fun and provides fantastic variation. High ledges, roofs and windows allow for sniping and vertical combat while the maze like passageways allow for close range fast-paced action. The connectivity of the map is excellent with everything intertwining and making weapons easily attainable, the only downside is that you will spend most of your time with absolutely no idea where you are and with no chance of finding where you want to be. The chosen weapons were good for the most part, but picking up the gluon gun can see you racking up the kills too easily, it would have been wise to leave it out.

Visually the map is very good. Most of the windows and doors are arched in some way, it shows that the author wasn’t lazy when building the map and made every effort to make it look good. Most corners have some sort of wooden support, adding to the setting of the map and generally making it look funkier. The textures were well detailed and suited the theme brilliantly. No new textures were included and the ones used were mostly from TF wads. All textures were compiled in the BSP.

The author has created an unmistakable mood in the map. Lighting has been wisely used to help gameplay and ambience. Many areas are quite dark, with only torches on some of the walls casting shadows everywhere. This means that it is not unlikely that there will be someone lurking in the darkness. There are never areas which are totally dark, and yet there are never any areas which are totally bright either. Thankfully there is no coloured light used as it would not have suited the theme, and could have also made the map very ugly if not used correctly. Sounds are used to create some nice effects. For example walking through the underground passageways cause an echo to radiate around the room. No new sounds were used in the map, however.

FINAL WORD
This a fairly short review because there isn’t a lot to complain about. It definitely deserved third place in the PHL mapping competition due to its great design, gameplay and lighting.

RATING: 8.3
visual: 8
ambient: 9
gameplay: 8
All ratings out of 10.0
